My name is Brian Loaeza Lorenzo, a student from University of California, Davis with a June 2019 graduation date. My degree is in Design, with an emphasis in Graphic and Web design. I have profeciencies in Adobe Illustrator, PhotoShop, After Effects, InDesign, and I am well-versed in HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Processing (java based language).

My interest in design all started when I was able to draw. I could never stop illustrating pictures and coloring a wide array of shapes and colors. For a long time it seemed as if I couldn't put my passion for drawing and visual creativity into an actual career. All I knew of the application of the arts were of artists selling portraits at some seaside pier, along with deceased famous people from an age when traditional art was more viable. It was only in community college that I realized that advertisments, websites, and logos were the modern application of the arts. I have been a designer since.

I really enjoy the art of story telling, and try to use my mediums (websites, posters, drawings) to engage viewers with a message. It is what makes graphic and website design rewarding; you go in with a concept in mind and then do your best to deliver it. I try to focus on form and color first, as I believe it is what draws people in and leaves the biggest impact. In my spare time I can be found illustrating fashion or concepts for future comic book projects. It is a personal dream of mine to complete a comic book project that has been stewing in my mind for almost a decade, once I finalize its artistic direction.

FontPoster

DES 16: Adobe Illustrator

In this project it was required that we select a famous/widely used font and research its history in order to make a poster. The poster was to reflect our research, as well as the its intended use. I designed the poster to feel reminiscent of a cinematic film intro, with bold large words for the title and small credit lines running in the background. This is becuase the font is frequently used in film intros and credit scenes. Friz-Quadrata is here to steal the show, so I made sure it was the main focus of the poster.

Tryptich: Symbolism of the Self

Intro to Computer Graphics: Adobe Photoshop

This assignment asked us to create a tryptich which represents ourselves, be it in a deep symbolic aspect or simply area of our interests. I elected to make the tryptich symbolic of who I am, my struggles, and aspirations. Most of my life I have felt caged, limited by my own sorrow and depression. However, I never saw myself as a being limited to my circumstances and condition. Rather, I had an innate optismism and creativity that allowed me to envision hope where I felt none - to see myself as powerfulr though I felt weak. The tryptich was to reflect these feelings, as well as illustrate my journey of self improvement as I live my life.

Immigration Lawyer Simulator

DES 157: HTML,CSS,JavaScript, BodyMovin Library Extension

For my interactive media ii class we had to do research on a topic regarding creativity or immigration. I chose immigration as my topic becuase I come from an immigrant background, and though I myself am not an immigrant my parents, however, are. Watching their struggles with the language and naturalizing themselves brought into question how accesible the information for naturalization really is. In my research and development, it became clear that the language used by the government to inform about the naturalization process was to complex and lengthy for someone with English as their second language. The robustness of the articles can be intimidating, and looking for help from proffessionals can induce fear in immigrants of being deported. My aim was to simplify it, and make an online virtual Lawyer who explains the process in a natural, simple, and inviting way from a safe space. While the project is incomplete for the time being, as we were only asked to produce a prototype, I aim to finish the project in the future.
